About me: I am a fourth-year PhD student in Statistics at Stanford University, where I am fortunate to be advised by John Duchi. My research interest lies broadly in the span of machine learning, optimization, and statistics. I am particularly interested in non-convex / non-smooth geometries and theories of deep learning.

I am delighted to complement my research with industrial experiences. I spent a wonderful summer at Google Research in 2016 working with Li Zhang.

Prior to Stanford, I was an undergrad in mathematics at Peking University.
